Turnover policy (optional)

What's this?Turnover policy

This policy describes how the turnover figure in your profit and loss account was calculated.

For example, 'The turnover represents amounts receivable for goods and services net of VAT and trade discounts'.

Turnover policy

Tangible fixed assets depreciation policy (optional)

What's this?Tangible assets depreciation policy

If you have tangible (physical) fixed assets, this policy describes how the depreciation (reduction in value) of each asset is calculated over its expected useful lifetime.

For example, 'Annual depreciation of computer equipment is calculated at 25% of cost to write off the cost of the asset, less its residual value, over its estimated useful economic lifetime'.

Tangible assets depreciation policy

Intangible fixed assets amortisation policy (optional)

What's this?Intangible assets amortisation policy

If you have intangible (non-physical) fixed assets, this policy describes how the amortisation (reduction in value) of each asset is calculated over its expected useful lifetime. Amortisation is similar to the depreciation value of a tangible (physical) asset.

For example, 'Annual amortisation of goodwill is calculated at 25% to write off the cost of the asset, minus its residual value, over its estimated useful lifetime'.

Intangible assets amortisation policy

Valuation information policy (optional)

What's this?Valuation information policy

If you have stocks or work in progress, this policy describes how they are valued. Stocks should be valued at the lower of either cost or ‘net realisable value’ (the realistic resale value minus the cost of selling it).

For example, 'Stocks and work in progress are valued at the lower of cost and net realisable value, after making allowances for obsolete and slow-moving items. Cost includes all direct expenditure and overheads'.

Valuation information policy
