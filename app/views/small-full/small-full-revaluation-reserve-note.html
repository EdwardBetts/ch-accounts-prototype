5 Balance sheet notes Revaluation reserve

Use this note to record how much assets have changed in value due to being revalued.

Complete all items that are included in your prepared accounts.


Surplus or deficit after revaluation

The total increase or reduction in the value of all tangible (physical) and intangible (non-physical) assets that were revalued in the period.

If assets reduced in value after a revaluation, enter a negative value using a minus sign. For example, if your company's machinery was valued at Â£10,000 less than the previous period, enter -10000.

 
Balance at 31 March 2017

The total revaluation reserve at the end of the period. This is calculated by adding together the revaluation reserve at the start of the period and any surplus arising from revaluations (or subtracting any deficit arising from revaluations).

This should match the revaluation reserve figure you entered in the balance sheet for the period you're filing for.
