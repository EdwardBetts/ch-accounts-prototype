These are the figures you entered on the balance sheet:

Tangible assets

Cost

Cost at 1 April 2015

The total cost or valuation of all intangible assets at the start of the period you're filing for.

Additions

The cost of all intangible assets acquired during the period you're filing for.

Disposals

The cost or valuation (at the date of disposal) of all intangible assets that were disposed of (for example, sold) during the period you're filing for.

Transfers

The cost or valuation of all intangible assets that have been transferred during the period you're filing for. If the asset is being transferred out, enter a negative figure using a minus sign.

Cost at 31 March 2016

The total cost or valuation of all intangible assets at the end of the period you're filing for. This is calculated by adding additions, revaluations and transfers to the cost or valuation of the assets at the start of the period, then subtracting any disposals.

Amortisation Amortisation is the reduction in value of an intangible (non-physical) asset over its useful lifetime. It's similar to the depreciation value of a tangible (physical) asset.

Amortisation at 1 April 2015

The total amortisation value of all intangible assets at the start of the period you're filing for.

Charge for year

The total amortisation charged against your profit for the period.

On disposals

The total amortisation value of all intangible assets that were disposed of (for example, sold) during the period you're filing for. This is the amortisation from the date each asset was acquired to the date of its disposal.

Other adjustments

Any other amortisation adjustment on all intangible assets.

Amortisation at 31 March 2016

The total amortisation value of all intangible assets at the end of the period you're filing for. This is calculated by adding intangible assets provided during the period, other adjustments and the amortisation value at the start of the period, then subtracting any disposals.

Net book value Net book value is the original cost of an asset minus any amortisation

Net book value at 31 March 2016

The net book value of all tangible assets at the end of the period you're filing for. This is the total cost or valuation of all tangible assets at the end of the period, minus the total amortisation value of all tangible assets at the end of the previous period.

Net book value at 31 March 2015

The net book value of all tangible assets at the start of the period you're filing for. This is the total cost or valuation of all tangible assets at the start of the period, minus the total amortisation value of all tangible assets at the end of the previous period.
